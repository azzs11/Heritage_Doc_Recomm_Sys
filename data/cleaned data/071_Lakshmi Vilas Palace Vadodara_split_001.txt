The Gaekwad Dynasty & Baroda State
The Gaekwads were originally Maratha chiefs under the Peshwas of Pune. In 1721, Pilaji Rao Gaekwad conquered Baroda (Vadodara) and made it their seat of power.
Over time, they became semi-independent rulers under the British, governing the princely state of Baroda (one of the wealthiest and most progressive states in India).
Maharaja Sayajirao Gaekwad III (1863–1939)
Gaekwad was the most prominent ruler of the dynasty and responsible for the Lakshmi Vilas Palace. He was adopted into the Gaekwad family after the previous ruler (Maharaja Malharrao) was deposed by the British for misrule. He ascended to the throne in 1875, when he was just 12 years old.
His Vision
He wanted Baroda to be a modern, progressive state, and introduced free and compulsory primary education (one of the first in India). Founded Maharaja Sayajirao University (MSU) of Baroda. Brought in industrial reforms, irrigation, and railways. Invited global experts in art, engineering, and education.
Construction of Lakshmi Vilas Palace (1878–1890)
Designed by Major Charles Mant, a British architect. Completed in 1890 at a cost of around ₹6 million (massive for that time). Built in the Indo-Saracenic Revival style, which mixes Hindu, Islamic, and Gothic European architecture. Covers 500 acres of land—including the palace, gardens, museum, golf course, and former private zoo.
Said to be the largest private residence built in the 19th century (four times bigger than Buckingham Palace).
